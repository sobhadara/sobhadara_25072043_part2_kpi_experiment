# Recommendation Memo

## Executive Summary

An A/B experiment was conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of a new onboarding and activation campaign for a subscription-based digital product. The objective was to determine whether the new onboarding experience should replace the existing onboarding process.

The analysis compared the performance of the Control and Treatment groups using the **Paid Conversion Rate** as the North Star Metric, supported by KPI analysis, hypothesis testing, and guardrail metric evaluation.

The results show that the Treatment group achieved a significantly higher Paid Conversion Rate than the Control group. Statistical testing confirmed that this improvement is significant, while the guardrail analysis identified a few operational risks that should continue to be monitored after rollout.

---

## North Star Metric

The North Star Metric selected for this experiment is **Paid Conversion Rate**.

Paid Conversion Rate measures the percentage of users who become paying subscribers after completing the onboarding journey.

This metric was selected because it directly reflects the primary business objective of increasing subscription revenue and has the strongest connection to business growth.

---

## KPI Tree Explanation

The KPI Tree was created to illustrate how the North Star Metric is influenced by key business drivers.

The three primary KPI drivers are:

- User Activation
- User Engagement
- Subscription Intent

These drivers are supported by the following sub-drivers:

### User Activation

- Trial Start Rate
- Onboarding Completion Rate

### User Engagement

- Average Engagement Score
- Average Days to Convert

### Subscription Intent

- Paid Subscription Rate
- Revenue per User

The KPI Tree also includes the following guardrail metrics:

- Support Ticket Rate
- Refund Rate
- Average Engagement Score

These guardrail metrics help ensure that improvements in conversion are achieved without negatively impacting customer experience.

---

## Experiment Result Summary

The Treatment group outperformed the Control group across most of the key business metrics.

Key observations include:

- Higher Landing Page Visit Rate
- Higher Trial Start Rate
- Higher Onboarding Completion Rate
- Higher Paid Conversion Rate
- Higher Average Revenue per User
- Higher Average Engagement Score
- Lower Average Days to Convert

These results indicate that users exposed to the new onboarding campaign progressed more effectively through the conversion funnel and became paying subscribers more quickly.

---

## Hypothesis Test Interpretation

A one-tailed Chi-Square Test of Independence was performed using Paid Conversion Rate as the primary metric.

The hypothesis test included:

- Observed frequencies
- Expected frequencies
- Chi-Square statistic
- Degrees of Freedom
- P-value
- Decision rule

The calculated p-value was less than the significance level of 0.05.

Therefore, the Null Hypothesis was rejected.

The statistical analysis provides evidence that the Treatment group achieved a significantly higher Paid Conversion Rate than the Control group.

---

## Guardrail Analysis

Improving conversion alone is not sufficient to recommend a rollout. Guardrail metrics were evaluated to ensure that the new onboarding experience did not introduce unacceptable business risks.

The following guardrail metrics were analyzed:

- Refund Rate
- Support Ticket Rate
- Average Days to Convert
- Average Engagement Score

The Treatment group demonstrated:

- Higher Average Engagement Score
- Faster conversion with fewer Average Days to Convert

However, the Treatment group also showed:

- Higher Refund Rate
- Higher Support Ticket Rate

These findings indicate that while the new onboarding experience improves conversion, customer support activity and refund behaviour should continue to be monitored after implementation.

---

## Segment-Level Insight

Performance was also evaluated across multiple user segments, including:

- Region
- Device Type
- Traffic Source

The Treatment group consistently achieved higher Paid Conversion Rates across most segments, indicating that the improvement was broadly distributed rather than limited to a single user segment.

This suggests that the new onboarding campaign performs well across different customer groups.

---

## Final Recommendation

**Recommendation: Launch**

Based on the experiment results, the new onboarding campaign should be launched.

The recommendation is supported by:

- Higher Paid Conversion Rate
- Stronger user engagement
- Faster conversion time
- Statistically significant improvement confirmed through hypothesis testing

Although Refund Rate and Support Ticket Rate increased slightly, these risks are manageable and do not outweigh the overall business benefits.

---

## Risks and Limitations

The following risks should continue to be monitored after rollout:

- Increase in customer support requests
- Increase in refund requests
- Changes in customer satisfaction
- Long-term user retention

In addition, the experiment measures short-term user behaviour. Continued monitoring is recommended to validate long-term business performance after implementation.

---

## Next Steps

The following actions are recommended:

- Launch the new onboarding campaign to all users.
- Continue monitoring Refund Rate and Support Ticket Rate.
- Track Paid Conversion Rate after rollout to ensure improvements are sustained.
- Monitor long-term customer retention and revenue.
- Conduct additional experiments to further optimize the onboarding experience and reduce operational risks.
